4. I think people with normal vision might find my dashboard accessible while it's more difficult for people with blindness or colorblindness to access. Inspired by dos and don'ts section of "A Comprehensive Guide to Accessible Data Visualization", in the design stage of my first chart, I delibrately used dropdown inputs instead of a fancy slider to receive year selection from users since a dropdown input can be used by screen readers. Also, for my pie chart, I added white strokes to separate between different portions to provide a more clear view.

5. I learned from "Why Accessibility Is at the Heart of Data Visualization" that one approach for presenting data as nonverbal sounds to blind people is called Sonification. However, I didn't add that feature in my dashboard since I am new in this area and I only have limited time to finish this assignment.
